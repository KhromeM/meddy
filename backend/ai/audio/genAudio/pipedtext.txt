The midday sun beat down as Maya, a young elephant calf, skipped ahead of the herd. She trumpeted, eager to reach the waterhole.  Her mother, trunk lovingly curled around Maya's, kept a watchful eye.  Suddenly, Maya tripped, her tiny tusk caught in a tangle of vines.  Her mother quickly freed her, a gentle rumble in her throat, reminding Maya to be careful.  Together, they rejoined the herd, their thirst soon to be quenched.
